bio

I’m a pharmacist and clinical informaticist with leadership experience across health systems and pharma. I focus on practical ways to use real-world data, health IT, analytics, and machine learning to improve outcomes for patients. My work has improved EHR-enabled clinical workflows at scale, including inpatient glycemic management, pain management, antimicrobial stewardship, clinical pharmacy services, pharmacogenetics, and other decision support. It has also contributed to earlier detection and treatment of conditions such as atrial fibrillation and cardiac amyloidosis, and to a better understanding of factors that influence treatment optimization and medication adherence, including the role of social drivers of health (SDOH). I’m a Fellow of the American Medical Informatics Association (FAMIA). I’ve published peer-reviewed research, and I continue to teach and mentor pharmacy students and residents as an adjunct clinical faculty member.
